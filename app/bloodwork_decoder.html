What is methylation?

Methylation is a biochemical process that happens over a billion times per second in every cell. It acts as an on/off switch for gene expression, detox, hormone metabolism, and brain chemistry by adding a methyl group (one carbon + three hydrogens) to DNA and proteins.

Why it matters for labs: When RBC, MCV, or MCH are elevated, it often signals B vitamins aren't being properly utilized — a methylation failure, not a deficiency. The body has the vitamin but can't convert it to its active usable form.

MTHFR gene mutation is the most common cause — it reduces folate conversion by 30–70%. Standard folic acid in most supplements is essentially useless for those affected. The C677T and A1298C variants are most common.

Signs of poor methylation: fatigue, brain fog, depression, anxiety, high homocysteine, elevated MCV/MCH, poor detox, chemical sensitivities, recurrent pregnancy loss.

What supports methylation: methylated B12 (methylcobalamin), methylated folate (5-MTHF), B6 (P5P form), riboflavin (B2), magnesium, zinc. Test for MTHFR via 23andMe or dedicated genetic panels.

The 8 recognized B vitamins — what each one does

The B vitamin family was originally numbered 1–16 but several were retired when researchers discovered they weren't essential vitamins. The 8 currently recognized:

B1 — Thiamine
Energy from carbohydrates. Critical for nerve and brain function. Depleted by alcohol and refined carbs.
B2 — Riboflavin
Required for MTHFR enzyme function — critical for methylation. Deficiency shows as cracked lips, light sensitivity, fatigue.
B3 — Niacin
NAD+ production for energy and DNA repair. Known to raise HDL cholesterol. High-dose causes a harmless "flush."
B5 — Pantothenic acid
Coenzyme A for fat/carb metabolism. Supports adrenal cortisol production — the "anti-stress vitamin."
B6 — Pyridoxine (P5P)
100+ enzyme reactions including serotonin, dopamine, GABA synthesis. Essential for hemoglobin. P5P is the active form.
B7 — Biotin
Hair, skin, nails, glucose metabolism. High-dose biotin (5mg+) falsely skews thyroid/hormone labs — stop 72hrs before any draw.
B9 — Folate (5-MTHF)
DNA synthesis, red blood cells, methylation. Folic acid ≠ folate — requires MTHFR conversion. Large red cells = folate/B12 issue.
B12 — Cobalamin (methylcobalamin)
Nerve myelin, DNA synthesis, methylation cycle. Vegans and low stomach acid = highest risk. Sublingual or methylcobalamin bypasses absorption issues.

Retired numbers: B4 (adenine), B8 (inositol), B10 (PABA), B11, B13, B15, B16, B17 — removed as they didn't meet criteria of essential human vitamins.

Iron Panel + Body Temperature Tracker
🌡 Thyroid → Iron Transport Connection

When thyroid function is low, body temperature drops below 98.6°F (37°C). Transferrin — the protein that transports iron through the blood — requires adequate body temperature and thyroid hormone activity to function. A cold, hypothyroid body physically cannot move iron efficiently, even when iron levels appear sufficient on labs. This is why many people with hypothyroidism remain iron deficient despite supplementing.

Target waking temp: 97.8–98.6°F. Below 97.8°F = suspect thyroid/adrenal issue affecting iron transport. Fix thyroid first or iron won't move.

Daily waking temperature log — take before getting out of bed
Reading your temperature:
98.6°F+ → Optimal thyroid/adrenal — iron transport working well
97.8–98.5°F → Mild hypothyroid signal — monitor iron transport + thyroid panel
<97.8°F → 🚩 Significant thyroid suppression — iron transport likely impaired
<97.0°F → 🚩🚩 Severe suppression — address thyroid before expecting iron to respond

My High-Dose Protocol

Vitamin D3 — High-Dose Rebuild Protocol

Why high-dose D3?

Standard dosing (2,000–5,000 IU/day) is insufficient when you've been deficient for years. D3 stores are depleted across fat tissue, bones, and organs — not just blood. Most people don't see meaningful improvement until they reach 60–80 ng/mL. The Bowles protocol uses high-dose D3 with cofactors to safely achieve and maintain optimal levels.

My schedule

Month 1: 25,000 IU/day — 5 capsules or 8 droppers
Month 2: 20,000 IU/day
Month 3: 15,000 IU/day
Maintenance: 10,000–15,000 IU/day ongoing
Standard dosing of 2,000–5,000 IU will not move the needle when truly depleted.

Critical cofactors — always pair with D3

MK-4 (K2) — daytime: Short half-life (~4 hrs). Take with D3. Directs calcium away from soft tissues into bones. Must dose multiple times daily.
MK-7 (K2) — nighttime: Long half-life (up to 72 hrs). Provides overnight and continuous coverage. Life Extension K2VITAL or BestVite K2VITAL — these are what I personally take.
Magnesium: Required to activate D3. Without it, D3 cannot convert to calcitriol — this is why some people supplement for years with no improvement.
Check labels: Some D3 supplements already contain K2. Verify before adding additional K2.

Magnesium — Types, Uses & My Recommendation

Why magnesium matters

Magnesium is involved in over 300 enzymatic reactions including D3 activation, ATP production, nerve function, and blood sugar regulation. Serum magnesium is a poor marker — only 1% of body magnesium is in blood. RBC magnesium is more accurate. Most people are severely intracellular-depleted with a "normal" serum level.

Magnesium glycinate (bisglycinate) ✓ Research-supported foundation
Highly bioavailable. Gentle on gut. Best for replenishing deficiency, anxiety, sleep, muscle recovery. Glycine supports liver detox and calming neurotransmitters.
Life Extension ZümXR Extended Release ✓ What I personally take
Slow-release bisglycinate technology. Releases over 8+ hours — prevents the loose stool side effect and maintains steady levels. What I personally use for long-term replenishment. Always consult your healthcare provider to find what works best for you.
Magnesium citrate
Good absorption. Mild laxative effect at higher doses. Useful for constipation. Not ideal for high-dose daily use.
Magnesium malate
Supports energy (Krebs cycle). Good for fibromyalgia, chronic fatigue, muscle pain. Gentle on gut. Take earlier in the day.
Magnesium threonate
Crosses the blood-brain barrier. Best for cognitive function and memory. Research shows it increases brain magnesium where other forms cannot reach.
Magnesium taurate
Excellent for cardiovascular health, blood pressure, heart rhythm, and glucose metabolism.
Magnesium oxide
Less than 5% bioavailability. Mostly a laxative. Common in cheap supplements. Avoid as a daily magnesium source.
Magnesium chloride (topical)
Applied to skin as oil or lotion. Bypasses gut. Good for localized muscle cramps and those who can't absorb oral forms.

Important: Always separate magnesium from iron by at least 2 hours — magnesium competes with iron for absorption.

Iron — Long-Term Deficiency Rebuild

When you've been deficient for years

Long-term deficiency depletes ferritin stores — these can take 6–12 months to fully rebuild even with consistent supplementation. The body's ability to transport iron is also compromised when thyroid function is low. Address thyroid in parallel — iron will not move in a cold body.

My iron protocol

Ferrex 150 daily — high-potency iron
Vitamin C with every iron dose — inhibits hepcidin and significantly increases uptake
Spacing: 4 hours from thyroid medications | 2+ hours from magnesium | Avoid calcium-rich foods and antacids
Monitor: %Transferrin saturation at every draw — if ≥70% stop iron immediately, retest in 30 days
